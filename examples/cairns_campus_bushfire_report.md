# Cairns Campus Bushfire Preparedness Draft Report

## Draft Notice

This is a demonstration report generated for the BushfireReadyGPT project. It is a planning support draft only. It does not provide live fire conditions, evacuation orders, fire bans, safe route confirmation or life-safety instructions. In an emergency, follow official emergency services and call `000` if life is at risk.

## Scenario

- Location: Cairns, Queensland
- Audience: Students, teachers and campus staff
- Setting: Campus bushfire preparedness planning
- Planning focus: Evacuation, assembly points, first aid training, role allocation, one-week action planning and official information sources
- Intended use: Controlled pilot discussion and preparedness planning review

## Purpose

The purpose of this plan is to help a Cairns campus prepare a structured bushfire readiness workflow before a fire event occurs. The plan supports internal planning, staff briefing and student safety awareness. It must be reviewed by campus management and relevant emergency authorities before any operational use.

## Key Planning Assumptions

- The campus is located in or near the Cairns local government area.
- Students and teachers may include visitors, international students and people unfamiliar with local emergency warning systems.
- Smoke, heat, power disruption and road congestion may affect campus operations.
- Official warnings and evacuation instructions must come from Queensland emergency services, local council channels and other authorised public information sources.

## Main Risks

1. Bushfire or grassfire threat near the campus or access roads.
2. Smoke exposure affecting students, staff and vulnerable people.
3. Confusion about assembly points, transport arrangements or communication channels.
4. Power, phone or internet disruption during a fast-moving event.
5. Unclear responsibility for student accounting, first aid and parent or guardian communication.

## Evacuation Principles

- Follow official emergency services and campus incident controller instructions.
- Move early where possible; avoid last-minute movement during smoke or road disruption.
- Keep evacuation routes visible in classrooms, shared facilities, residences and administration areas.
- Maintain accessible evacuation support for students, staff and visitors with mobility, language or health needs.
- Treat route selection as a live operational decision that must be confirmed by authorised personnel.

## Assembly Point Planning

Candidate assembly points should be confirmed through campus management and relevant local emergency guidance.

Potential assembly point types:

- Large indoor spaces such as a gymnasium, hall or multipurpose building.
- Open outdoor areas away from vegetation, fuel loads, vehicle movement and smoke concentration.
- Off-site fallback locations identified with local authorities or partner organisations.

Each assembly point should be checked for:

- Capacity for students, staff and visitors.
- Access for emergency vehicles.
- Shade, drinking water and basic amenities.
- First aid access and AED visibility.
- Clear signage and backup communication options.

## Roles And Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
| --- | --- |
| Campus Incident Controller | Activates the preparedness plan, coordinates with emergency services and approves internal messaging. |
| Evacuation Wardens | Guide people from buildings to assembly areas and report blocked routes. |
| Teachers And Supervisors | Keep class groups together, support student movement and report missing or injured people. |
| First Aid Lead | Sets up first aid support, checks first aid kits and coordinates trained responders. |
| Communications Lead | Sends internal alerts, maintains parent or guardian messaging and records official information sources used. |
| Student Support Lead | Supports students with accessibility, language, anxiety or medical needs. |
| Facilities Lead | Checks signage, exits, water, generators, fire equipment and site access constraints. |

## First Aid And Training

The campus should maintain a first aid training plan covering:

- Smoke inhalation awareness.
- Heat stress and dehydration response.
- Burns first aid.
- AED location and basic use.
- Student accounting and welfare checks.
- Support for people with asthma, disability, anxiety or language barriers.

Training should be paired with evacuation drills and after-action reviews. Training records should identify who is trained, when training occurred and when refreshers are due.

## One-Week Action Plan

| Day | Action |
| --- | --- |
| Day 1 | Confirm responsible owners for emergency coordination, evacuation, first aid, communications and facilities. |
| Day 2 | Review campus maps, candidate assembly points and accessibility needs. |
| Day 3 | Check signage, first aid kits, AED locations, water access and backup communications. |
| Day 4 | Prepare draft student, staff and parent or guardian communication templates. |
| Day 5 | Run a tabletop exercise using a Cairns bushfire smoke and access disruption scenario. |
| Day 6 | Update the draft plan from exercise feedback and confirm outstanding risks. |
| Day 7 | Produce a reviewed preparedness pack for campus leadership approval. |

## Official Information Sources To Verify

- Queensland Fire Department / Queensland Fire and Emergency Services entry points for Queensland fire and emergency guidance.
- Queensland Government disaster and warning channels.
- Cairns Regional Council disaster and emergency management information.
- Bureau of Meteorology fire weather and severe weather warnings.
- Triple Zero (`000`) for life-threatening emergencies.

These sources should be checked directly during preparedness review and during any real event. This project can list source entry points but does not replace official warnings or emergency instructions.
